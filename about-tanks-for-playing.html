Summary

Tanks for Playing is a compact demo showcasing a simple tank versus AI duel implemented using the HTML5 canvas and vanilla JavaScript. The aim is to be the last tank alive. The demo emphasizes small, readable game logic, a basic AI, and client-side persistence so it can run without a server.

How to play

  • Movement/rotation: Arrow keys or WASD — rotate and move the player's tank.
  • Shoot: Space bar to fire.
  • Objective: Survive and eliminate the enemy tank. Matches typically need to last at least ~10s to be counted by the scoring logic.

How it works

Game loop — The animation loop updates objects (tanks, bullets) each frame, resolves collisions, and draws the scene to the canvas.

Collision & combat — Bullets and tank collisions are detected using simple circle/rectangle checks. Damage reduces tank health; when a tank reaches zero it is disabled and the match ends.

AI — The enemy tank attempts to face the player and shoot when aligned, with simple movement and decision logic to create approachable but capable opponents.

Stats — Tanks have tunable stats: speed, range, turnSpeed, fireRate, health and damage. Note: some builds transform values (e.g. logarithms) when computing effective cooldown/damage.

Upgrades & powerups

The demo includes a simple mechanism to apply a +1 upgrade to one tank stat (speed, range, turnSpeed, fireRate, health, damage). The operation mutates the localStorage tank object.

Leaving the page

Leaving counts as a loss to prevent chickening out of a losing battle. The fuller system handled this more elegantly.
